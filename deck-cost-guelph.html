How Much Does a Deck Cost in Guelph?

A clear breakdown of 2026 deck pricing in Guelph and Wellington County.

The honest answer: most new decks in Guelph fall between $8,000 and $28,000, and where you land depends mostly on size, material, and railing choice. Here is how it breaks down.

Typical price ranges

  • Pressure-treated deck (12x16 ft, basic railing): $8,000–$14,000.
  • Composite deck (same footprint, aluminum or cable railing): $18,000–$28,000.
  • Multi-level, curved, or pergola-integrated decks: higher, quoted per project.

What drives the cost

Five things move the number more than anything else:

  • Size — square footage is the biggest single factor.
  • Decking material — pressure-treated is the budget option; composite (Trex, TimberTech) costs more upfront but eliminates staining.
  • Height above grade — taller decks need more substantial posts, footings, and guards.
  • Railing — pressure-treated railing is cheapest; aluminum, glass, and cable cost more.
  • Site conditions — sloped yards, poor soil, and tricky access add labour.

Pressure-treated vs composite over time

Pressure-treated lumber is roughly half the cost of composite up front, but it needs cleaning and re-staining every 1–2 years. Composite costs more initially and essentially eliminates maintenance for decades. Over a 15-year horizon the lifetime cost is often closer than it first appears.

Permits and design

Most Guelph decks require a building permit, and that cost (plus drawings) should be in your budget. We handle the permit application and provide the design drawings as part of our service — see our permits & design page.

How to get an accurate number

Online calculators only get you in the ballpark. We provide a detailed written quote after a free on-site design consultation, so the price reflects your actual yard, access, and material choices.
