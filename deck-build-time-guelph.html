How Long Does a Deck Take to Build in Guelph?

From first call to finished deck — what to expect on timing.

Construction itself is fast — a straightforward deck is usually built in 3 to 5 days. The longer part is permit approval, which adds 2 to 4 weeks before we can start. Here is the full timeline.

Typical timeline

  • Free consultation & design: a few days to lock in layout, material, and a written quote.
  • Permit approval (City of Guelph): 2–4 weeks.
  • Construction: 3–5 days for a standard deck (footings, framing, decking, railing).

What can slow it down

  • Multi-level or curved designs, or integrated pergolas and lighting.
  • Difficult site access, rock, or poor soil at the footing locations.
  • Peak-season permit and inspection backlogs in spring and early summer.

What speeds it up

Booking the consultation early (so the permit is in the queue before peak season), choosing a standard rectangular layout, and having materials selected before submission all shorten the calendar.

Best time of year to build in Guelph

Decks can be built almost year-round here, but spring books up fast. Reaching out in late winter often means your deck is ready for the first warm weekends. Fall is an underrated, lower-wait window.
